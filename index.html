Concept Intervention

We insert a transparent concept layer into large language models, enabling researchers to read, edit, and control model behavior at inference time — no retraining required.

Architecture Pipeline

Encode
LLM hidden rep. h ∈ ℝd
↓
2
Concept Bottleneck Layer
Project h → A ∈ ℝk
↓
3
Intervene
A′ = DαA · edit concepts
↓
4
Predict
Linear head on A′ → logits

Key Contributions

What We Built & Found

Four concrete results advancing interpretable, steerable language model deployment.

01
📊
Reproduced CB-LLM Baselines
Validated adversarial training on SST-2, YelpP, AG News, and DBpedia, confirming substantial steerability gains with minimal accuracy cost.
0.57 → 0.82 steerability on SST-2
02
🎯
Single-Neuron Causal Control
Zeroing one Sports neuron drops accuracy by 33 points, confirming a causal — not merely correlational — bottleneck structure.
0.94 → 0.61 accuracy (Sports suppressed)
03
🧠
Multi-Neuron Coordination
Suppressing clusters of semantically related neurons amplifies prediction shifts beyond single-neuron edits, enabling targeted behavioral correction.
Coordinated suppression → larger Δy
04

Experimental Results

Adversarial Training Unlocks Steerability

Without adversarial training the bottleneck leaks class information around concept neurons. With it, steerability reaches 0.95 on YelpP while accuracy stays within 0.3% of Llama 3 baseline.
